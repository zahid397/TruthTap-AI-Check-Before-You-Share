# Problem Statement — TruthTap AI

## The one-sentence problem
Young people are the heaviest sharers of online content, yet they have the fewest built-in habits and tools to check whether that content is true before they pass it on.

## The context
Social media is now the front door to news for young people. According to the Pew Research Center (2025), **76% of adults aged 18–29 get news from social media at least sometimes** — the highest of any age group. At the same time, trust in that information is fragile: only about half of young adults say they have *a lot of / some* trust in what they see on social platforms.

This creates a dangerous gap. The generation that shares the most is navigating an information environment that is harder to verify than any before it:

- **AI-generated images and deepfakes** now look real. Extra fingers and melted text are getting rarer as generators improve.
- **Fabricated “screenshots” of trusted outlets** spread faster than the corrections. During the April 2025 Spain–Portugal blackout, fake headlines styled like real newspapers falsely blamed sanctions on Russia — the outlets never published those stories.
- **Emotional and urgent framing** (“BREAKING”, “share before it's deleted”, “doctors warn”) is engineered to bypass critical thinking.
- **Health, money and political claims** — the most harmful categories — travel through family group chats with zero friction.

A 2026 study of 667 content creators (News Creator Corps) found **“Misinformation and Trust” ranked as their #1 challenge (57%)** — ahead of burnout, AI and monetization. If professional creators struggle with trust, everyday young users are even more exposed.

## Why existing options fall short for youth
- **Professional fact-checking sites** are excellent but slow, text-heavy, and often feel “not for me” to a teenager mid-scroll.
- **Generic AI chatbots** can help, but they require access, cost money at scale, can hallucinate, and don't teach a repeatable habit.
- **“Just be more careful” advice** doesn't work without a concrete, low-effort action to take in the three seconds before hitting share.

## The consequences of the gap
When unverified content is shared:
- **Misinformation spreads** to thousands within minutes.
- **Panic** results from fake emergencies (cancelled exams, fake outages, false health scares).
- **Reputations are harmed** by manipulated images and deepfakes.
- **Trust erodes** — in institutions, in media, and between people.

## Our problem framing
The core issue isn't a lack of information — it's a lack of a **pause**. The single most powerful anti-misinformation behavior is stopping to check *before* sharing. But that pause needs to be:

1. **Fast** — usable in seconds, on a phone.
2. **Non-judgmental** — it should coach, not scold.
3. **Explainable** — it must show *why*, so users learn.
4. **Accessible** — free, no login, no jargon, works everywhere.

## What TruthTap AI sets out to do
Give every young person a simple, friendly “pause button” for the internet:

> **Paste → Check → Understand → Decide.**

A tool that scores content, explains the risk in plain language, traces the source, suggests real verification steps, and — through a built-in quiz — teaches the Media & Information Literacy skills to eventually not need the tool at all.

## Target of change
- **Primary:** youth aged 13–30, especially students, in any country, on any smartphone.
- **Secondary:** educators and youth organizations who can use TruthTap AI as a classroom MIL activity.
